Usage

Activating

To activate the item press [SHIFT + RMB]. Same can be done to turn it off. If a player throws (or fatfingers [Q]) an active saber from their mainhand it automatically turns off.

Attacking

When the item is active, it does 12hp of damage when left-clicked on an entity (or player). However like any other saber item, this can be countered via Blocking. A left-click can also deflect any incoming(in parry range, or in 3 block radius for this specific item) projectiles (given that the projectile is in a somewhat linear path, unlike shulkers for example), but timing would have to be precise.

Blocking

Rotary Saber, like any other saber item, when right-clicked, will block any damage coming from a saber item, and even projectiles if timed properly. Can be held down for about ~1.75 seconds which if exceeded will cause the projectile to do damage. A visual indicator is now shown when in a hanging block, and the duration has been fine-tuned. Critical hits have been removed for balance, the parry range has been fine-tuned to better suit the weapon, and trident parrying has been fixed.

Boomrang

Pressing [SABER-ABILITY] key throws the rotary saber like a boomrang, once it hits an entity or a block it will return back to you. This will temporarily disable blocking after you pick it up. This ability can be parried and blocked, and it is followed by a cooldown of 80 ticks (4 seconds) before the next throw can be performed. If the player is not in range (for example, offline, in another dimension, or the chunk is not loaded) the thrown rotary saber will crash into anything and drop as an item. Entities in the way of the thrown rotary saber take repetitive 2hp damage, regardless of whether it returns, and a proper hit does the same 12hp damage as a melee attack.

Flight

Looking up and holding down right-click will toggle flight as long as the item is held down. Flight is better suited for scaling vertically, like mountains for example, or if you just skill issue into the void. Flying does mean the user is vulnerable to any melee and ranged attacks. If a player is shot while flying they will stop flying. A flying player can only fly for 200 ticks (10 seconds) after which flight disables and they start falling. It will take them about 40 ticks (2 seconds) to fly again if their flight has ended, canceled or disabled. Another way to counter a flying player is to activate Disruption.

HUD & Tooltips (v0.0.4)

Small sprites (HUD hints) are now displayed near the crosshair to indicate the saber's ability status, such as whether an ability is on cooldown, in use (duration), or ready to use. Furthermore, pressing [SHIFT] while hovering over the item in the inventory displays detailed ability tooltips.
